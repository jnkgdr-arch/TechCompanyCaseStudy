Tool selection & implementation

What worked, what broke, and why

The AI-enhanced platform replaced spreadsheets, Microsoft Project files, and the legacy PPM system with a centralized portfolio environment. Benefits were strongest where workflows aligned, managers were engaged, and data inputs were clean.

Executive Decisions

Current constraint

Selective ELT, inconsistent inputs, and missing history prevent reliable cross-portfolio AI recommendations.

Governance fix

Assign data stewards, standardize templates and naming conventions, and audit quality on a recurring cadence.

Interim architecture

Use functional data marts and SAN storage while the full enterprise warehouse is implemented.

AI decision support analysis

Potential vs. actual utilization

Gap diagnosis

The system could have supported lifecycle tracking, budgeting, predictive risk analysis, scenario planning, and cross-department coordination. Actual utilization was constrained by 60% adoption, fragmented history, and low trust in illogical or biased recommendations.

Ethical & organizational impact

Responsible AI controls

Bias audits

Test whether resource and schedule recommendations favor departments with cleaner historical data.

Human oversight

Require managers to approve material budget, staffing, and timeline changes before execution.

Privacy controls

Limit staff-profile access, monitor data handling, and enforce governance committee review.

Transparency

Show assumptions, confidence levels, missing data, and recommended mitigation steps on every AI insight.

Recommended executive roadmap

90-day stabilization plan

Days 1–30

Diagnose

Run department-level workflow diagnostics, data-quality scoring, and stakeholder training needs analysis.

Days 31–60

Govern

Launch data stewards, standard templates, ETL validation rules, and AI bias review procedures.

Days 61–90

Optimize

Deploy role-based dashboards, explainable AI panels, change logs, and adoption coaching.
